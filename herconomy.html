Herconomy App

Role: UX Designer

Savings App for Women

Herconomy is an app that helps women save and earn interest, invest and also get discounts when they buy from affiliate stores. Users get up to 10% per annum on their savings. The app has 2 main features. The auto savings which is called Vault, where you automatically save frequently and have only 4 withdrawal days yearly to encourage the habit of saving frequently the second main feature is called Float, which can also be referred to as a flexible wallet where you can withdraw at any time and has no breaking fee. We started the project with a competitive analysis, as providing uniqueness was our key objective here. One of the challenges I faced as the lead designer of the Herconomy app & web app was to make it stand out from other competitors while keeping the amount of data clean, simple, and easy to navigate on any device.

Personas

The insight I got from the interviews I conducted led me to create two personas which led me to empathize with users.

Becky

Age: 20 Location: Lagos, Nigeria Occupation: Student/Writer

A 20-year-old final year student of Lagos state university and also a writer. She does writing gigs to earn extra money aside from the monthly stipend from her parents. Her primary goal is to ace her final year exams and then save enough money to rent an apartment for herself.

Frustrations & Fears
  • Wants an automated saving system.
  • Keeps going back to spend from her savings.
Sarah

Age: 45 Location: Lagos, Nigeria Occupation: Office Manager

Sarah is a busy working mother of two teenage children. She works as an office manager at a small accounting firm and is responsible for managing the finances of the business. In her free time, Sarah enjoys cooking and spending time with her family. Sarah is looking for ways to save money. She wants to find an app that can help her save for big expenses like family vacations and her children's college education.

Frustrations & Fears
  • Sarah is often short on time and has a lot of competing priorities.
  • She also has a hard time finding the time to research different financial products and determine which ones are the best fit for her needs.
